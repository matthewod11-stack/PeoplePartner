### SCENE 4 — Going Deeper
**Video time:** 0:32–0:52
**Source clip:** Recording 1:28–2:00 (frames 45–60)

**Screen:**
1. **(3 sec)** Kai Cruz's profile visible in the right panel (rating 2.0, eNPS 7, 15y 8m tenure, three declining ratings). The AI response is streaming with **Historical Excellence** — "Maintained exceptional ratings: 4.6 → 4.5" and **Concerning Recent Decline** — "Dramatic rating drop: 4.5 → 2.0 (55% decrease in one quarter)."
2. **(3 sec)** Show the **Engagement Data Correlation** — "eNPS trend: Consistently passive but declining (8 → 8 → 7)" and the key concern from Q4 2024: *"Workload increasing without added support."*
3. **(3 sec)** The follow-up question: "I'd like to have a conversation with Kai, how should I approach it?" appears in a teal bubble.
4. **(5 sec)** AI response streams: **Pre-Conversation Preparation** with key metrics to reference, then **Recommended Conversation Structure** — "Lead with Strengths & Data Recognition" with a scripted opener: *"Kai, I've been reviewing your performance data, and your track record is impressive — $42K in cost savings, consistent efficiency improvements."*
5. **(3 sec)** Show **Diagnostic Questions** — *"What's different about your role since December?"* and **Success Metrics to Track** — Immediate, 30-day, 90-day.
6. **(3 sec)** Hold on the **Critical Point**: *"Frame this as data-driven problem-solving, not performance management. A 13-year exceptional performer doesn't suddenly develop foundational skill gaps — there's likely a systemic cause."*

**Voiceover:**
> "The AI doesn't just report numbers. It correlates performance with engagement data, spots the pattern — workload concerns from a survey showing up as a rating decline — and coaches you through the conversation. It even drafts your opening line, referencing the employee's actual contributions. This isn't generic HR advice. It's advice about *your* people."

**Edit notes:** This is the "wow" scene. The side panel showing Kai's 4.5→2.0 decline while the AI discusses it is the visual proof that the system understands the data. Hold on the scripted opener — that's the moment viewers realize this is different.
